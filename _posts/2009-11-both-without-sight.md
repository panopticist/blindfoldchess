A fascinating variation of blindfold chess occurs when both players play without sight of the board. These rare games present unique challenges and have produced memorable encounters throughout chess history.

With neither player able to see the position, the game becomes a true test of pure calculation and memory. This blog examines notable double-blindfold games and the players who excelled at this extremely difficult form of chess.
