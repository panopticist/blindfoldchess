Not everyone in chess history has approved of blindfold chess. A 1906 chess columnist expressed strong concerns about the mental strain of playing without sight of the board, arguing that it could be harmful to players' health.

This blog examines historical attitudes toward blindfold chess, including the medical and chess authorities who worried about its effects. The debate about whether blindfold chess is dangerous continued for decades.
