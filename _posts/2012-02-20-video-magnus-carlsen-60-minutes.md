CBS 60 Minutes featured a segment about chess prodigy Magnus Carlsen, including impressive demonstrations of his blindfold chess ability.

The program showcased Carlsen's extraordinary memory and visualization skills, which are fundamental to blindfold chess mastery. Even at the highest levels of competition, Carlsen's ability to play without sight of the board stands out among world champions.

This report helped bring blindfold chess to mainstream attention and demonstrated how this remarkable skill continues to fascinate both chess players and the general public.
